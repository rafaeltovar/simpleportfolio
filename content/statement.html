Occupied spaces

A squat is a space that allows collectives and groups to come together and live autonomously, independent of institutions. It works as an assembly and allows people to design their own lives, on both a personal and communal level, under their own conditions and not those imposed by neoliberal politics.

These photos show a small set of occupied spaces. These photos want to show the collective construction of spaces to live, meet and create. Spaces very different from each other but with a common axis: Occupied spaces.

These pictures were taken in Cordoba, Malaga and Madrid (Spain) in 2011.

Rafael Tovar, February 2013
